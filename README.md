Executive Summary
PNP Lite is a consumer-focused group-buying community designed to give final consumers access to wholesale pricing through coordinated bulk purchasing.
 The model leverages PacknPay’s distribution network, procurement strength, and community-based demand aggregation to create value for both the business and consumers.
PNP Lite will run as a paid membership community, operating primarily through clusters in Port Harcourt, Lagos, and Abuja. Members will enjoy highly discounted product prices while PacknPay increases sales volume, customer retention, and brand trust.

Operational Framework
A. Project Lead (Head of Business / Project Manager)
Oversees PNP Lite strategy and implementation


Approves product list, pricing, and cycle timelines


Signs off on operational changes and escalations


B. Community Manager
Manages WhatsApp community & cluster groups


Coordinates announcements, price releases, updates


Onboards new members


Handles FAQs and member engagement


Reports weekly member activities


C. Procurement Lead (P-connect)
Sources all products from verified suppliers


Negotiates and locks in bulk prices


Ensures product availability for each cycle


Works with the warehouse to schedule offloading and preparation


D. Warehouse & Fulfillment Unit
Receives products


Handles de-bulking into consumer-ready quantities


Quality checks


Sorting and packaging for each city cluster


Handover to the logistics unit


E. Logistics Coordinator (P-connect)
Schedules deliveries per cluster


Manages dispatch riders and delivery hubs


Tracks delivery status


Handles delivery complaints


F. Finance & Administrative Unit
Confirms payment for membership and orders


Manages installment plan tracking


Updates the member database


Tracks referral bonuses


Produces weekly reconciliation reports


Oversees revenue recognition and cost allocation


 Order Cycle Structure (Weekly or Bi-Weekly)
1. Price Release
Community Manager releases the weekly price sheet


Includes available products, quantities, and deadlines


2. Order Window
Members place orders using a simple WhatsApp form (this is for the pilot phase) 


Orders auto-sync into a central Google Sheet


3. Payment Confirmation
Members immediately pay for selected items


Finance tags orders as PAID, PENDING, or INVALID


4. Procurement Execution
Procurement buys required bulk quantities


Delivery to the warehouse for sorting


5. Fulfillment & Packaging
Warehouse teams de-bulk items


Package based on order list


Label by cluster and delivery routes


6. Dispatch
Nearby cluster deliveries via bikes


Longer-distance deliveries via buses


Delivery fee borne by the customer


Logistics coordinator posts delivery updates per cluster


7. Cycle Closure
Finance reconciles sales & payments


Team reviews complaints, challenges & improvements


Next cycle preparation begins
